# Translation SEO by Market — Search Term Research

Research conducted March 2026 via Etsy market pages, eRank keyword data, and competitor analysis. Focused on what buyers actually type into search in each language for handmade rusty metal garden art.

---

## Key Insight: "Edelrost" Is the #1 German Keyword

The German term **"Edelrost"** (noble rust) is the single most important keyword for this niche. It appears on nearly every garden decoration category page on Etsy.de and signals *intentional artistic rust* rather than neglected metal. This is not a direct translation of any English term — it's a German-specific concept that buyers actively search for.

**Action:** Use "Edelrost" in German titles and tags for every product with rust patina. It replaces generic translations like "rostig" or "verrostet" which carry negative connotations.

### German Compound Words Are Tags

German buyers type compound nouns as single search terms. These are highly valuable because they're specific and low-competition:

- **Rostkugel** (rust ball) — not "rostige Kugel"
- **Gartenstecker** (garden stake) — single word, 13 chars
- **Feuerskulptur** (fire sculpture) — 13 chars
- **Pflanzstecker** (plant stake) — 13 chars
- **Beetstecker** (flower bed stake) — 11 chars
- **Rostsäule** (rust column) — 9 chars
- **Metallpflanze** (metal plant) — 13 chars

**Action:** Prefer compound German words over multi-word phrases. They fit within the 20-char tag limit more easily and match how Germans actually search.

### Competitor Insight: Metallmichl and Edelrostshop

German Etsy competitors like Metallmichl and Edelrostshop dominate with these category terms. Their top-performing tags include:
- "Edelrost Gartendeko"
- "Cortenstahl Kugel"
- "Gartenstecker Edelrost"
- "Rost Gartenfigur"

**Action:** Don't try to compete on identical generic terms. Differentiate with "handgemacht Spanien" (handmade Spain), specific product names, and long-tail combinations they don't cover.

### Key French Insight: "Fer Forgé" Carries Weight

"Fer forgé" (wrought iron) is a high-trust term in France with strong artisan connotations. Even though the products are welded steel, the general "metal forgé" or "fer forgé" adjacent terms resonate with French buyers looking for handcrafted metalwork.

### "Fait Main Espagne" as Differentiator

French buyers on Etsy.fr value provenance. "Fait main en Espagne" or "fait main Espagne" is a differentiating tag that no German or Dutch competitor can use.

**Action:** Include "fait main Espagne" as a tag on every French listing.

### German Cross-Search on Etsy.fr

The German term "edelrost gartenstecker" has its own active Etsy.fr market page, meaning German-speaking buyers in border regions (Alsace, Switzerland, Belgium) also shop on Etsy.fr.

**Action:** Consider adding 1-2 German crossover tags to French listings targeting border-region buyers.

### Critical: Do NOT Default to Wabi-Sabi

Slapping ワビサビ (wabi-sabi) on everything rusty is a cliché and culturally lazy. Wabi-sabi is a deep philosophical aesthetic, not a marketing tag for Spanish metal art.

**When 枯山水 (zen garden) IS appropriate:**
- Tsuba garden stakes (actually Japanese-inspired)
- Abstract minimalist sculptures
- Items specifically designed for meditative garden settings

**When it is NOT appropriate:**
- Coasters, candle holders, lamps, chess stakes, bonfires — these have nothing to do with zen or wabi-sabi

### What Actually Works for Japanese Buyers

- **男前インテリア** (masculine interior) — a real, active Japanese interior design trend for raw/industrial/metal decor. Maps perfectly to candle holders, coasters, lamps, and industrial pieces.
- **スペイン製** / **欧州製ハンドメイド** (made in Spain / European handmade) — carries genuine cachet. Japanese buyers value imported European artisan goods.
- **ガーデンオブジェ** (garden object) — the standard Japanese search term for decorative garden pieces.

**Action:** Match Japanese tags to the product's actual aesthetic, not a stereotyped assumption about what Japanese buyers want.

## Cross-Market Tag Strategy

### Universal Tags (translate per language)

These concepts should appear across all languages, adapted to local search terms:

1. **Material**: corten steel / Edelrost / acier corten / cortenstaal / acciaio corten
2. **Handmade provenance**: handmade in Spain / handgemacht Spanien / fait main Espagne / hecho a mano
3. **Product category**: garden sculpture / Gartenskulptur / sculpture jardin / scultura giardino
4. **Use case**: outdoor decor / patio / terrace / garden
5. **Gift angle**: gardener gift / housewarming / anniversary

### Tags That Should NOT Be Translated Literally

| English | Bad literal translation | Better local term |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| rustic | rústico / rustique | Edelrost (DE), patine (FR) |
| yard art | arte de patio | Gartendeko (DE), art jardin (FR) |
| lawn sculpture | escultura césped | Gartenskulptur (DE), sculpture jardin (FR) |
| weathered steel | acero erosionado | Cortenstahl (DE), acier corten (FR) |
| zen garden | jardín zen | 枯山水 (JA), Zen Garten (DE) |

### Tag Length Budget per Language

Some languages are more verbose than others. Plan tag lengths accordingly:

| Language | Avg word length | Fit within 20 chars | Strategy |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|----------|
| German | Long (compound words) | 1-2 words | Use compound nouns: Rostkugel, Gartenstecker |
| French | Medium | 2-3 words | Compact phrases: "sculpture jardin", "art métal" |
| Spanish | Medium-long | 2-3 words | Drop articles: "escultura jardín" not "escultura de jardín" |
| Italian | Medium-long | 2-3 words | Same as Spanish: drop prepositions where possible |
| Dutch | Medium | 2-3 words | Compound words help: "tuinbeeld", "cortenstaal" |
| Japanese | Short (dense) | 3-5 chars/word | Katakana loanwords are longer; kanji is compact |

## Seasonal Tag Adjustments

Based on EU seasonal calendar (see `03-eu-market-strategy.md`):

| Season | Add These Tags | Remove/Deprioritize |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|

| **Sep-Oct** (autumn) | "Herbstdeko" (DE), "déco automne" (FR), autumn garden | Summer leisure terms |
| **Nov-Dec** (holiday) | Gift terms in all languages, "Weihnachtsgeschenk" (DE), "cadeau Noël" (FR) | Generic decor terms — replace with gift-focused |
| **Jul-Aug** (dead season) | N/A — use this time to refresh tags for autumn | Don't waste renewals |

**Action:** Schedule quarterly tag refreshes aligned with EU seasonal calendar. Tags are free to change — use this.
