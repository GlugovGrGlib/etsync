# EU-to-US Selling Guide

How to sell effectively from the EU to US buyers on Etsy. Covers shipping, pricing, legal/tax, customs, and marketing adjustments.

---

## Shipping: Carriers and Costs

### Carrier Comparison

| Carrier | Speed (EU→US) | Best For | Cost Level |
|---------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| National postal (La Poste, Deutsche Post, bpost) | 10-21 days | Small/light items under 2 kg | Cheapest |
| DHL eCommerce / DHL Packet | 7-14 days | Best all-around for most packages | Moderate |
| Asendia | 7-14 days | Lightweight parcels, competitive rates | Moderate |
| DHL Express | 2-5 days | High-value items, rush orders | Expensive |
| FedEx International Economy | 5-8 days | Heavy/bulky items | Expensive |
| UPS Standard | 5-8 days | Heavy/bulky items | Expensive |

### Recommended Setup

- **Default (most items):** DHL eCommerce or Asendia — best balance of cost, speed, and tracking
- **Small/light items (under 500g):** national postal service tracked packet
- **High-value or rush:** DHL Express — fast but only when the item value justifies the cost
- **Heavy items (sculpture, planters):** FedEx/UPS if DHL eCommerce won't take the weight

### Cost Optimization

1. **Minimize package dimensions** — carriers charge by dimensional weight or actual weight (whichever is greater). Use the smallest box possible.
2. **Use a shipping platform** — Sendcloud (EU-focused, aggregates carrier rates) or Packlink Pro (integrated with Etsy). Both provide discounted rates vs. retail counter pricing.
3. **Negotiate contract rates** — if shipping 50+ parcels/month, contact DHL or UPS for business account rates. Discounts of 30-60% off retail are common.
4. **Calculate your flat rate** — ship 10-20 packages, track actual costs, compute the average, add 5-10% buffer. Review and adjust quarterly.

### Delivery Time Expectations (Set in Listings)

| Service Level | Delivery Estimate | When to Use |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Economy (postal) | 14-25 business days | Budget option |
| Standard (DHL eCommerce) | 10-18 business days | Default |
| Express (DHL Express) | 3-7 business days | Premium option |

Add 2-5 days for customs clearance to all estimates. During holiday season (Nov-Dec), add 5-10 extra days.

**Always** message buyers after shipping with tracking info and realistic timeline.

### Tracking

- Etsy's Star Seller program requires tracked shipping on 95% of orders
- Without tracking, you have weak protection in "item not received" disputes
- At minimum use services that provide a tracking number, even if end-to-end tracking is limited
- DHL eCommerce and Asendia both provide tracking that works across borders

### Packaging for Fragile Items (Art, Sculpture, Ceramics)

| Method | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| **Rigid mailer** | Prints and flat art. Add "Do Not Bend" sticker. |
| **Tube** | Large prints. Roll with tissue paper to prevent curling. |
| **Double-boxing** | Originals, sculpture, ceramics. Inner box with cushioning inside outer box. |
| **Expanding foam packs (Instapak)** | Irregular sculpture shapes. Conforms perfectly. |
| **Corner protectors** | Framed art. Also tape glass in star pattern so shards stay contained if broken. |

**Always insure** high-value or fragile items. DHL, FedEx, and national posts all offer insurance. Third-party options like Shipsurance (integrated with Etsy) can be cheaper.

## Shipping Profiles Setup

### Rules for EU Sellers

1. **Never use Etsy's "calculated shipping"** — it only works with US carriers (USPS, UPS, FedEx). It cannot access your EU carrier rates. The calculated amounts will be wrong.
2. **Use flat-rate shipping profiles** — calculate your average cost per weight/size tier and set a flat rate.
3. **Create a dedicated US shipping profile** — don't rely on a generic "everywhere else" profile.
4. **Create separate profiles per product type** — a lightweight print ships differently than a heavy ceramic.
5. **Offer tiered rates** — "First item $12 shipping, each additional item $3" encourages multi-item orders.
6. **Offer an express upgrade option** — some US buyers will gladly pay more for faster delivery.

### Example Shipping Profile Structure

```
Profile: "Art Prints (US)"
  - First item: $8.00
  - Additional item: $2.00
  - Processing time: 1-3 business days
  - Delivery estimate: 10-18 business days

Profile: "Small Sculpture/Decor (US)"
  - First item: $18.00
  - Additional item: $5.00
  - Processing time: 3-5 business days
  - Delivery estimate: 10-18 business days

Profile: "Heavy Items (US)"
  - First item: $35.00
  - Additional item: $10.00
  - Processing time: 3-5 business days
  - Delivery estimate: 10-18 business days

Profile: "Express Upgrade (US)" [as variation add-on]
  - Add $25.00 for DHL Express (3-7 business days)
```

## Pricing for the US Market

### Price in USD

**Set your Etsy shop currency to USD** if the US is a major target market.

Why:
- US buyers see exact prices you set, not awkward conversions (EUR 47 → ~$51.23)
- You control pricing psychology — clean price points ($25, $49, $75)
- Etsy charges ~2.5% currency conversion fee either way (converting your EUR payout from USD sales, or converting USD display prices from your EUR shop currency). No fee difference.
- You receive payouts in EUR after Etsy converts — but your listed prices are exactly what you intended

**Exchange rate risk:** EUR/USD fluctuates between ~1.03 and ~1.12. Build a 5% buffer into pricing and reprice quarterly.

### Shipping Cost Strategy

| Item Price | Shipping | Recommended Approach |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| Under $30 | Would be >30% of price | Charge shipping separately, keep it reasonable |
| $30-75 | 10-25% of price | **Absorb into item price, show "free shipping"** |
| Over $75 | <15% of price | Absorb into price, free shipping |
| Heavy items (sculpture, planters) | >$30 | Charge shipping separately — buyers understand |

Etsy's algorithm gives a ranking boost to free-shipping listings, especially for US buyers. The "free shipping guarantee" threshold is $35 for US orders.

### Competitive Positioning

**Do NOT compete on price or shipping speed with US domestic sellers.** You will lose.

Instead, compete on:
- **Quality and craftsmanship** — "Handcrafted in [your country]" carries premium cachet
- **Uniqueness** — "You won't find this in any US store"
- **Story and heritage** — the artisan, the tradition, the region
- **Materials** — "Murano glass," "Limoges porcelain," "Czech crystal" have instant US recognition

**US buyers pay 20-40% premium** for European handmade goods in categories like ceramics, textiles, leather, art, and jewelry. Price accordingly — do not underprice.

## Customs, Duties, and Tariffs

### HS Codes for Production Sculpture/Decor

Multiple copies of the same design (even if handmade) are classified as decorative articles by material, NOT as original artwork:

| Product Type | HS Code | Base MFN Duty |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Art prints | 4911 | ~0-3% |
| Ceramic sculpture/decor | 6913 | ~0-6% |
| Metal statuettes/decorative objects | 8306 | ~0-5% |
| Wood decorative objects | 4420 | ~3-5% |

**Do not use HS 9703** (original sculpture, duty-free) for production runs. It only applies to unique one-of-a-kind pieces.

### Trump Tariffs: What You Must Know

**The tariff situation for EU→US shipments has changed drastically. Research the CURRENT rates before selling to US.**

What happened in 2025:
- April 2, 2025: 20% "reciprocal tariff" announced on EU goods
- April 9, 2025: 90-day pause, reverting to 10% baseline
- July 2025: pause extended and renegotiated — rates continue to evolve. **Check current rate before selling.**
- The $800 de minimis exemption was eliminated for Chinese goods (May 2025) and then for ALL countries (August 2025)

**Total duty a US buyer could face = base MFN duty + reciprocal tariff.** Rates change frequently — always verify current rates before pricing.

**Where to check current rates:**
- US HTS search: https://hts.usitc.gov/ (shows current duties including executive order modifications)
- US CBP: https://www.cbp.gov (de minimis status, enforcement changes)
- USTR: https://ustr.gov (trade negotiations, tariff announcements)
- Etsy Seller Handbook and community forums (real-time seller reports)

### Customs Declaration Rules

1. **Always declare accurately** — fill out CN22 (under ~EUR 300) or CN23 (higher values)
2. **Use the correct HS code for your product type** — see table above. Wrong codes cause clearance delays.
3. **Mark as "merchandise"** not "gift" — US customs penalizes misrepresentation
4. **Never under-declare value** — illegal, risks seizure

### What This Means for Your Shop

**Do not assume buyers will pay no duties.** Even small parcels may now face tariffs.

- **Courier shipments (DHL Express, FedEx, UPS):** the carrier acts as customs broker and collects duties from the recipient plus a brokerage fee. This creates surprise charges and friction.
- **Postal mail (Correos → USPS):** enforcement on small parcels is tightening with the end of de minimis.

**What to do:**
1. State clearly in shop policies: "US buyers are responsible for any import duties and tariffs. These are set by US customs and may be significant — please check current US tariff rates on EU goods before ordering."
2. For high-value items via courier: consider offering DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — you pre-pay the duty so the buyer gets no surprises. Factor the full cost (item + shipping + duty) into your price.
3. Consider whether the US market is worth pursuing at current tariff rates, or whether to focus effort on EU where there are zero customs barriers.
4. Monitor the situation — tariff rates can change by executive order with little warning.

## Legal and Tax

### VAT

- **Exports to US are zero-rated for VAT** — you do not charge VAT on US sales
- You can still reclaim input VAT on materials/supplies used for exported goods
- Keep proof of export (shipping receipts, customs declarations, tracking confirmations)
- **IOSS is irrelevant** for US exports — it's only for imports into the EU

### US Sales Tax

- **Etsy collects and remits US sales tax automatically** as a "marketplace facilitator"
- You do NOT need to register for US sales tax
- Sales tax is collected separately from the buyer — it does not reduce your payout
- You may need to fill out a **W-8BEN form** for Etsy's records (non-US person declaration)

### Income Tax

- Revenue from US sales is taxable income in your home EU country, same as domestic sales
- Keep records of all sales, fees, shipping costs, and expenses
- Etsy provides annual tax summaries

## Marketing to US Buyers

### Write in American English

| American English | Not This (British) |
|------------------|--------------------|
| color | colour |
| jewelry | jewellery |
| gray | grey |
| customize | customise |
| fall | autumn |
| apartment | flat |
| sweater | jumper |

### Use US Measurements First

Always list inches/feet/pounds first, metric in parentheses:
- "24 x 36 inches (61 x 91 cm)"
- "Weighs 5 lbs (2.3 kg)"

US buyers do not intuitively understand centimeters.

### Tone

American e-commerce copy is warmer and more benefit-focused than typical European styles. Emphasize how the product makes the buyer **feel**, not just what it is.

**Instead of:** "Stoneware vase, 20 cm, glazed in celadon."
**Write:** "This hand-thrown stoneware vase brings calm, organic beauty to any room. The celadon glaze catches morning light beautifully. 8 inches tall."

### US Holidays Calendar (Key Shopping Events)

| Event | Date | Relevance |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Valentine's Day | Feb 14 | Jewelry, art, personalized gifts |
| Mother's Day | 2nd Sunday in May | One of the biggest Etsy events |
| Father's Day | 3rd Sunday in June | Moderate |
| Independence Day | Jul 4 | Not a gift holiday |
| Halloween | Oct 31 | Growing decor market |
| Black Friday / Cyber Monday | Late Nov | Massive — run Etsy sales |
| Christmas | Dec 25 | The big one. Post international shipping deadlines early. |

**Key difference from EU:** Mother's Day is in May (not March as in UK/Ireland). Start marketing 6-8 weeks before each event, accounting for international shipping time.

### Timing

- **Peak US browsing:** 6pm-10pm Eastern (midnight-4am CET) and 8am-10am Eastern (2pm-4pm CET)
- **List/renew during US peak hours** for the recency boost
- **Schedule social media posts** for US time zones using Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite
- **Respond to messages within 12 hours** — US buyers expect fast responses. Check during your evening (their business hours).

### Leverage "Made in Europe"

1. **Be specific:** "Handcrafted in a family pottery studio in Tuscany" beats generic "Made in Europe"
2. **Name materials with recognition:** "Murano glass," "Czech crystal," "Swedish linen"
3. **Show heritage:** "This technique has been used by artisans in [region] for over 200 years"
4. **Show your workshop and landscape** — authenticates the European origin story
5. **Sustainability angle:** EU environmental standards are perceived as higher. Mention REACH compliance, organic certifications, sustainable sourcing.
6. **Exclusivity:** "You won't find this in any US store"

## Etsy Settings Checklist for EU→US Sellers

- [ ] Shop currency set to USD (if US is primary market)
- [ ] "United States" explicitly enabled in every shipping profile
- [ ] Shop and listings written in English
- [ ] Dedicated US shipping profile per product category (flat rate, not calculated)
- [ ] Express shipping option available as upgrade
- [ ] Processing times realistic (pad by 1-2 days)
- [ ] Shop policies address: customs duties, international returns, delivery timeline
- [ ] About section includes your location, studio photos, and origin story
- [ ] W-8BEN form submitted to Etsy
