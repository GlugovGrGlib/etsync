# Category Playbooks: Art, Sculpture, Handmade Decor, Garden Decor

Each section covers pricing, photography, descriptions, seasonality, shipping, differentiation from mass-produced competition, and 2025-2026 trends.

---

## 1. Art Pieces (Originals, Prints, Digital, Mixed Media)

### Pricing

| Type | Price Range | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Small originals (<12") | $50-150 | Start lower, raise as reviews accumulate |
| Medium originals (12"-24") | $150-500 | Sweet spot for most buyers |
| Large originals (24"+) | $500-2,000+ | Statement pieces, longer sales cycle |
| Art prints (standard sizes) | $15-45 | Giclee prints command 30-50% more |
| Large/limited-edition prints | $45-120 | Number them ("12/50") for scarcity |
| Digital downloads | $3-15 single, $15-35 bundles | Volume game, high margin |

**Tactics:**
- Use **.99 pricing for prints** ($24.99) — charm pricing works for commodity-like items
- Use **round numbers for originals** ($350, $500) — signals quality and artistry
- **Always have a few high-priced originals** in your shop, even if they rarely sell — they make mid-range items look affordable (price anchoring)
- Offer every design in **3 sizes** — the medium option sells most (decoy effect)

### Photography Checklist

- [ ] Photo 1: Art piece clean and clear, full view, natural diffused light
- [ ] Photo 2: Lifestyle mockup — art framed on a styled wall in a room
- [ ] Photo 3: Texture/detail close-up showing brushstrokes or material quality
- [ ] Photo 4: Scale reference (ruler, hand, or furniture nearby)
- [ ] Photo 5: Packaging or process shot (you creating the work)
- [ ] Video: 5-15 sec slow pan across surface showing texture (increases conversion significantly)

**For prints:** flat lays on clean neutral surface work. Mockups are acceptable and expected.
**For originals:** real photos strongly preferred over mockups. Show the actual piece.

### Description Template

```
[Emotional opening — what inspired this piece, how it makes a space feel]

[2-3 sentence artist's statement — your intention, the story behind the work]

Details:
- Dimensions: [H x W inches (H x W cm)]
- Medium: [e.g., acrylic on stretched canvas]
- Surface: [stretched/unstretched, framed/unframed]
- Signed: [yes/no, front/back]

Shipping:
- Prints ship flat in rigid mailer / rolled in tube
- Originals ship with corner protectors, bubble wrap, double-boxed
- Processing time: [X] business days
- Delivery estimate: [X-X] business days (US), [X-X] business days (EU)

[If applicable: "Frame not included. Arrives ready to hang / ready to frame."]

[CTA: "Message me for custom sizes or color palette adjustments."]
```

### Seasonal Strategy

| Season | What to Push | What to Create |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Jan-Feb | Minimalist, abstract, "refresh your space" messaging | Spring/botanical inventory |
| Mar-May | Florals, botanicals, nature, bright colors | Summer landscapes |
| Jun-Aug | Travel/landscape art, digital downloads (steady) | Q4 holiday inventory |
| Sep-Oct | Fall palettes, warm tones, gallery wall sets | Holiday gift listings |
| Nov-Dec | Prints, digital downloads, personalized/custom pieces | Announce commission cutoff by Oct 1 |

### Shipping

- **Prints:** rigid mailers or tubes. $4-8 domestic, $8-15 EU-to-US.
- **Originals on canvas:** corner protectors + bubble wrap + double-box. Always insure originals over $100.
- **Sell unframed** to reduce shipping cost and breakage risk. Recommend buyers frame locally.
- **Customs:** original artwork enters the US duty-free under HS chapter 97 (code 9701). This is a significant advantage — use it.

### Differentiation Tactics

1. Include a **signed Certificate of Authenticity** with every original (costs nothing, adds perceived value)
2. **Number limited-edition prints** ("12/50") — scarcity drives urgency
3. **Show your hands/process** in at least one listing photo and on social media
4. Offer **custom color palettes or sizes** — mass producers cannot do this
5. Use "handmade" and "original" in titles and tags — buyers actively filter for these

### 2025-2026 Trends

- **Textured art** — heavy impasto, plaster art, 3D texture on canvas. "Textured wall art" search volume growing fast
- **Maximalism returning** — bold colors, layered gallery walls, eclectic mixing
- **Earth tones** — terracotta, sage, ochre, cream dominating palettes
- **Nostalgia/retro** — 70s-inspired palettes and vintage botanical illustrations
- **Eco art** — recycled materials, natural pigments, eco-friendly packaging
- **AI backlash = opportunity** — buyers increasingly seek verifiably human-made art. Document your process.

## 2. Sculpture (Ceramic, Metal, Wood, Mixed Media)

### Pricing

| Type | Price Range | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Small decorative (<8") | $30-120 | Impulse-buy range, gift-friendly |
| Medium statement (8"-18") | $120-500 | Core offering for most sellers |
| Large/commissioned | $500-5,000+ | Long sales cycle, high margin |
| Functional ceramics (vases, bowls) | $25-80 | Higher volume, lower margin |
| Metal sculpture (tabletop) | $80-250 | Commands premium |
| Wire art / lightweight | $30-100 | Easy to ship, trending |

**Do not underprice sculpture.** Buyers on Etsy expect to pay more for 3D handmade work. Underpricing signals low quality.

**Formula:** (Hourly rate x hours) + (materials x 2-3) + shipping buffer. Target $25-50/hour equivalent.

### Photography Checklist

- [ ] Photo 1: Front view, clean background, soft directional light from 45 degrees
- [ ] Photo 2: 3/4 angle showing dimensionality
- [ ] Photo 3: Side view
- [ ] Photo 4: Back view
- [ ] Photo 5: Detail close-up — glaze, weld marks, wood grain, texture
- [ ] Photo 6: Scale reference — next to hand, book, coffee mug, or in room setting
- [ ] Photo 7: In context — on shelf, mantle, desk, or in garden
- [ ] Video: slow 360-degree rotation on turntable (build a simple turntable for $15-30)

**Lighting:** single diffused light source from 45 degrees creates drama and emphasizes form. Flat lighting flattens dimensionality — avoid it.

### Description Template

```
[Material and technique lead: "Hand-thrown stoneware, fired to cone 6" or "Forged mild steel with patina finish"]

[Process narrative: "Each piece is hand-carved from a single block of walnut over approximately 40 hours."]

[What makes it unique: "This exact piece is the one you will receive" for originals. "Each piece is handmade and will have slight variations" for production.]

Details:
- Dimensions: [H x W x D inches (cm)]
- Weight: [X lbs (X kg)]
- Material: [specific — "high-fire stoneware with celadon glaze," not just "ceramic"]
- Care: [food safe? outdoor safe? will it develop patina? cleaning instructions]

Shipping:
- Double-boxed with 2" minimum cushioning on all sides
- Insured for full value
- Processing: [X] business days
- Delivery: [X-X] business days

[CTA: "I accept custom commissions — message me to discuss your vision."]
```

### Shipping (This Is the Hardest Category)

- **Always double-box** with minimum 2" cushioning on all sides
- **Use expanding foam packs** (Instapak) for irregular shapes — packing peanuts shift in transit
- **Wrap in soft material first** (tissue, fleece), then bubble wrap, then rigid box
- **Do NOT offer free shipping** on heavy items — charge calculated or flat-rate based on weight
- **A 15-pound sculpture costs $25-60 domestic, $80-200+ EU-to-US.** Price accordingly.
- **Items over 30 lbs or 36" in any dimension** may need freight shipping. Be upfront.
- **Breakage policy:** state clearly in policies — "If your item arrives damaged, photograph the damage AND packaging within 48 hours and contact me." File carrier claims promptly.
- **Carrier choice:** UPS and FedEx handle heavy/fragile better than postal services. DHL Express for high-value international.
- **Customs:** For production runs (multiple copies of the same design), use the correct HS code by material — 8306 for metal, 6913 for ceramic, 4420 for wood. HS 9703 (original sculpture, duty-free) only applies to unique one-of-a-kind pieces. The $800 de minimis exemption was eliminated in 2025 — US buyers now face duties on all shipments.

### Differentiation

1. **Show your studio** — kiln, forge, carving bench, raw materials
2. **Material sourcing stories** — "Clay from a local deposit," "Reclaimed agricultural steel"
3. **Document commission process** — concept sketch to finished piece portfolio
4. **Artist bio with training/credentials** — sculpture buyers are often art-aware

### 2025-2026 Trends

- **Ceramic renaissance** — organic, imperfect "wabi-sabi" forms dominating
- **Concrete/terrazzo** — modern planters, bookends, decorative objects
- **Wire sculpture** — minimalist line art (faces, figures, continuous line). Easy to ship.
- **Resin art** — ocean art, resin-wood hybrids
- **Body forms** — abstract torsos, face sculptures, bust sculptures
- **Kinetic sculpture** — wind-driven, balanced pieces. Niche but high-value.
- **Corten (rusted) steel** — industrial-natural contrast, very popular in contemporary design

## 3. Handmade Decor (Wall Art, Textile, Decorative Objects)

### Pricing

| Type | Price Range | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Small wall hangings (macrame, woven) | $20-50 | Entry-level, gift-friendly |
| Medium wall hangings | $50-150 | Core offering |
| Large wall hangings | $150-400 | Statement pieces |
| Decorative objects (vases, trays, candle holders) | $15-80 | Volume items |
| Mirrors and frames | $30-200 | Size-dependent |
| Clocks, shelves, functional decor | $25-120 | Practical appeal |

**Tactics:**
- **Bundle 3-5 items** as "gallery wall sets" or "room collections" at 10-15% discount vs. individual — increases average order value significantly
- **Offer same design in 3 sizes** (small/medium/large) — the medium sells most
- **Gift wrapping add-on** — small charge, high margin, especially around holidays

### Photography Rules

- **Styled room shots are mandatory** — handmade decor must be shown in context
- **Consistency** — same color palette, lighting, and editing style across all listings
- **"Instagram-worthy" styling** — plants, books, candles as props
- **Before/after** — blank wall vs. wall with your piece installed
- **70% lifestyle, 30% product-only** — this ratio converts best for home decor
- **Natural light only** — artificial lighting reads as cheap for decor photography

### Description Keywords That Convert

Use interior design style terms in descriptions and tags — these are what buyers search:
- Bohemian / boho
- Mid-century modern
- Scandinavian / Nordic
- Farmhouse / rustic
- Coastal
- Japandi (Japanese-Scandinavian fusion)
- Minimalist
- Maximalist / eclectic

Always suggest specific rooms: "Perfect for a living room accent wall, bedroom nook, or nursery."

### Shipping

- **Flat/rolled items** (textile hangings): ship flat or rolled. Rolled macrame bounces back to shape.
- **Fragile decor** (ceramic, glass): same double-box principles as sculpture.
- **Oversized but lightweight** items (large macrame): beware dimensional weight pricing. Use the smallest box possible.
- **Consider designing items to ship disassembled** with clear assembly instructions — reduces cost and breakage.
- **Branded packaging** — tissue paper, thank-you card, care instructions. The unboxing experience drives reviews and repeat purchases.

### 2025-2026 Trends

- **Plaster/textured wall art** — joint compound on canvas, extremely popular on social media
- **Arched and organic shapes** — mirrors, shelves, wall art in non-rectangular forms
- **Mushroom motif** — mushroom-shaped decor, lamps, vases (has legs into 2026)
- **Dopamine decor** — bold, colorful, maximalist. Color is back after years of neutral.
- **Sculptural candles** — twisted, knotted, shaped candles as decorative objects
- **Japandi** — clean lines, natural materials, muted tones with warm wood
- **Moss and living art** — frames/structures for growing moss, succulents, air plants

## 4. Garden Decor (Outdoor Art, Planters, Sculpture, Yard Art)

### Pricing

| Type | Price Range | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Small garden stakes/markers | $8-25 | Volume items, gift-friendly |
| Planters (ceramic/concrete, small-medium) | $20-80 | Core offering |
| Large statement planters | $80-200 | Premium, heavy to ship |
| Garden sculpture (small-medium) | $40-200 | Good margin |
| Large garden sculpture | $200-1,000+ | High value, complex shipping |
| Metal yard art (spinners, stakes, silhouettes) | $25-120 | Trending, moderate shipping |
| Garden signs and markers | $10-30 | Easy to ship, customizable |
| Stepping stones | $15-50 each, sets at slight discount | Heavy |

**Garden buyers are less price-sensitive than indoor decor buyers.** They're investing in outdoor living. Do not underprice.

### Photography Checklist

- [ ] Photo 1: Item in an actual garden setting with plants visible
- [ ] Photo 2: Different angle in garden
- [ ] Photo 3: Close-up of material/texture/finish
- [ ] Photo 4: Scale reference with plants or garden features nearby
- [ ] Photo 5: Planters shown WITH plants in them
- [ ] Photo 6 (if applicable): Weathering/patina example — what it looks like after a season outdoors
- [ ] Video: item outdoors in natural light, possibly in wind (for kinetic pieces)

**Lighting:** morning or golden hour. Avoid harsh midday sun.

### Description Must-Haves

Weather resistance is the **#1 buyer concern** for garden items. Address it prominently:

```
Weather Resistance:
- Material: [e.g., "14-gauge steel with powder-coated finish"]
- Designed for: [year-round outdoor use / 3-season / indoor-outdoor]
- UV resistant: [yes/no]
- Frost resistant: [yes/no]
- Maintenance: ["Seal annually" or "No maintenance — develops natural patina"]

For planters:
- Drainage holes: [yes/no/can be drilled]
- Frost-safe: [yes/no]
- Fits plant pot size: [X inches]
- Indoor/outdoor: [both/outdoor only]

Installation:
- [Stakes into ground / Sits on flat surface / Wall-mounted / Hangs]
- Hardware included: [yes/no / what's included]
- Weight: [X lbs — important for wind resistance and handling]
```

**Gift angle:** "Makes a wonderful gift for gardeners, nature lovers, or anyone who loves unique outdoor decor." Garden items are popular gifts — mention it.

### Seasonal Strategy (This Category Is Highly Seasonal)

| Month | Sales Level | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Feb-Mar | Ramp-up | **List all spring items by February.** Buyers plan ahead. |
| Apr-Jun | **Peak (40-60% of annual sales)** | Full inventory live, maximum promotion, Pinterest push |
| Jul-Aug | Moderate | Summer/patio-specific items. Shade garden accessories. |
| Sep-Oct | Second peak | Fall garden, harvest themes, Halloween-adjacent |
| Nov-Jan | Slow | Create inventory, refresh listings, plan spring. Bird feeders/evergreen items still sell. |

### Shipping (Most Challenging Category)

- **Heavy + fragile + irregularly shaped** = the worst combination for shipping
- **Concrete/ceramic planters:** extremely breakage-prone. Custom foam inserts or expanding foam packs. Double-box always. Consider offering local pickup.
- **Metal sculpture:** more durable. Wrap sharp edges/points to prevent puncturing packaging. Heavy-duty boxes.
- **Do NOT offer free shipping on heavy garden items.** A 25-pound concrete planter costs $30-70 to ship domestically.
- **Use UPS/FedEx** for heavy items (better handling than postal services).
- **Items over 100 lbs or 4 feet:** LTL freight. Be transparent about the delivery process — freight requires someone to receive at a truck.
- **International shipping:** garden items are rarely worth shipping internationally due to weight/cost/breakage. Many successful garden sellers restrict to domestic only.
- **Offer shipping tiers:** "First item $12 shipping, each additional item $3" — encourages multi-item orders.

### Differentiation

1. **Durability proof** — "I've had pieces in my own garden for 5+ years" with photo evidence. Single most powerful differentiator from cheap imports.
2. **Wildlife-friendly function** — pieces that double as bee hotels, bird feeders, butterfly habitat. Functional art is a strong niche.
3. **Customization** — personalized garden signs, custom-sized planters, bespoke sculpture for garden themes
4. **Sustainability** — "Made from reclaimed barn wood," "Recycled steel." Garden buyers are nature-oriented.

### 2025-2026 Trends

- **Concrete planters** — geometric, modern, terrazzo-style (embedded chips)
- **Corten steel** — rusted steel garden art and edging, industrial-natural contrast
- **Fairy/miniature gardens** — still going strong, cottagecore aesthetic
- **Native plant accessories** — pollinator garden accessories, rain garden items, meadow-style plantings
- **Gothic garden** — gargoyles, dark metalwork, moody color planters (growing niche)
- **Outdoor living room** — decor that blurs indoor/outdoor boundaries
- **Moss and living art** — structures for growing moss, succulents, air plants as living walls
- **Bestselling sculpture types:** raw metal dandelion stems, kinetic art, metal spheres, whimsical ceramic stakes, zen monk statues, rusty farmhouse metal

## Cross-Category Commission Process

If you accept custom orders, create a dedicated "Custom Order Deposit" listing with this process:

1. **Initial consultation** (free) — buyer describes what they want
2. **Concept sketch/proposal** — you send a sketch or mockup with price quote
3. **50% deposit payment** — non-refundable after work begins (state this clearly)
4. **Creation with progress photos** — send updates at milestones
5. **Final approval** — buyer confirms or requests revisions (1-2 included, more at hourly rate)
6. **Balance payment** — remaining 50%
7. **Shipping**

**Pricing custom work:** charge **20-40% more** than comparable non-custom pieces. Custom requires more communication, revisions, and carries higher dissatisfaction risk.

**Seasonal cutoffs:** announce clearly — "Last day to order custom pieces for Christmas delivery: November 1." Creates urgency and manages expectations.
