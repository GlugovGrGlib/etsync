# EU Market Strategy

Primary market strategy for an EU-based Etsy shop (Spain) selling handmade sculpture, art, and garden decor to EU buyers.

---

## EU Markets by Priority

| Market | Priority | Why | Language |
|--------|----------|-----|----------|
| **Germany** | #1 | Largest EU Etsy market. Absorbed DaWanda's user base (2018 migration). High spending on handmade. | German translations essential |
| **France** | #2 | Second largest. Strong artisan culture ("fait main"). | French translations essential |
| **Netherlands** | #3 | High Etsy adoption per capita. Excellent English proficiency. | English OK, Dutch optional |
| **UK** | #4 | Largest non-US market overall, but post-Brexit customs friction. Focus on items under 135 GBP. | English |
| **Italy** | #5 | Growing market, strong home/garden decor interest. | Italian translations helpful |
| **Spain** | Domestic | Smaller Etsy market, but no shipping friction. | Spanish |
| **Austria, Belgium, Scandinavia** | Secondary | Reached via German/English listings. | German/English |

### What EU Buyers Value Differently

- **Sustainability is expected, not optional** — eco-friendly packaging, recycled materials, local sourcing. Especially German, Dutch, and Scandinavian buyers.
- **Quality over quantity** — fewer purchases, higher expectations. "Built to last" beats "trendy and disposable."
- **Shipping cost transparency** — free shipping is less expected in EU than US. Buyers accept reasonable costs but want them upfront, no surprises.
- **Delivery patience** — 5-10 business days intra-EU is acceptable for handmade. German buyers are more demanding (Amazon Prime conditioning).
- **Product safety** — GPSR compliance expected. Proper product information matters.
- **Smaller living spaces** — EU apartments and houses are smaller. Small-to-medium sculpture and decor sells better than oversized statement pieces.
- **Intense garden culture** — EU gardens are smaller but MORE intensively decorated. Quality garden pieces are highly valued, especially in Germany, Netherlands, UK.

## Language Strategy

### Etsy Multi-Language Listings

Etsy offers manual translation fields that override auto-translation. Use them — auto-translations produce awkward results for artisan terminology.

Manually translated listings generally convert better in the target language market than auto-translated ones.

### Priority

1. **English** — primary listing language (base for all markets)
2. **German** — translate titles, descriptions, and tags for your #1 market
3. **French** — translate for your #2 market
4. **Spanish** — for domestic market
5. **Italian** — if capacity allows

### What to Translate

- Titles and tags (critical for search in that language)
- First 2-3 paragraphs of description (where the keywords and selling points are)
- Dimensions — keep both metric (primary for EU) and imperial (for any US browsers)

### Translation Tips

- Do not machine-translate tags — research what German/French buyers actually search for
- "Handgemacht" (German) not just "handmade" translated literally
- "Gartendeko" / "Gartenskulptur" / "Pflanzgefäß" — learn the actual search terms in each language
- Use Etsy's search bar in German/French Etsy (.de / .fr) to see autocomplete suggestions

## Shipping from Spain

### Carrier Comparison

| Carrier | Speed (intra-EU) | Cost (1kg, Germany) | Tracking | Best For |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|----------|----------|
| **Correos Internacional** | 5-10 days | ~10-15 EUR | Basic, loses visibility at borders | Budget, lightweight items |
| **Correos Express** | 3-5 days | ~18-25 EUR | Better end-to-end | Higher-value, time-sensitive |
| **DHL** | 3-6 days | ~15-22 EUR | Excellent, trusted by German buyers | Germany-bound orders |
| **GLS** | 3-5 days | ~12-18 EUR | Good | Central Europe |
| **DPD** | 3-5 days | ~14-20 EUR | Good | France/Benelux |
| **MRW** | 2-3 days domestic | N/A international | Good domestic | Spanish orders only |

### Use Packlink or Sendcloud

Both are shipping aggregators that integrate with Etsy and compare rates across carriers per shipment:

- **Packlink** — headquartered in Spain, strong Correos/DHL/GLS/SEUR integration. Often cheaper than going direct.
- **Sendcloud** — strong across EU, good DHL/DPD rates.

Set up one of these instead of using a single carrier. Saves 15-30% on average.

### Shipping Cost Ranges from Spain

| Destination | Small parcel (<1kg) | Medium parcel (1-5kg) |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Domestic Spain | 4-8 EUR | 8-15 EUR |
| France, Portugal | 8-15 EUR | 15-25 EUR |
| Germany, Benelux, Italy | 10-18 EUR | 18-30 EUR |

### Shipping to UK (Post-Brexit)

The UK is a large market but comes with friction:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Customs declaration | Required (CN22 under 300 EUR, CN23 above) |
| VAT on orders under 135 GBP | Etsy collects and remits automatically |
| VAT on orders over 135 GBP | Buyer may be charged import VAT on delivery |
| Duty | Depends on HS code and value |
| Risk | Buyers sometimes refuse delivery due to unexpected charges |

**Strategy:** Focus on items priced under 135 GBP for UK. Clearly state in listings: "UK buyers: Etsy handles VAT on orders under 135 GBP. Orders above this amount may incur additional import charges."

## VAT and Tax Compliance

### Etsy and VAT (EU)

Since July 2021, Etsy is the "deemed supplier" for B2C sales in the EU:

- **Etsy charges, collects, and remits VAT** to EU tax authorities on your behalf
- You do NOT charge VAT to the end buyer — Etsy handles it
- You receive the net amount (sale price minus VAT and Etsy fees)
- This applies to both cross-border EU sales AND domestic (Spain-to-Spain)

You still need to report Etsy sales in your Spanish tax declarations. Etsy provides sales summaries.

### Spanish Autónomo Obligations

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Registration | Seguridad Social (autónomo) + Hacienda (IAE, Modelo 036/037) |
| Monthly contribution | Progressive by income. ~230-260 EUR/month minimum. Tarifa plana (~80 EUR/month for first 12 months if new) |
| IAE epígrafe | Likely 861 (sculptor) or similar artistic activity code |
| Quarterly declarations | Modelo 303 (IVA), Modelo 130 (IRPF estimated payment) |
| Annual declarations | Modelo 390 (IVA summary), Modelo 100 (income tax) |
| Invoicing | Required. VeriFactu digital invoice verification is being phased in 2025-2026 — consult gestoría |

**Get a gestoría experienced in e-commerce/marketplace sellers.** Budget 100-200 EUR/month. The intersection of Etsy marketplace facilitator rules and Spanish autónomo obligations is complex.

## GPSR Compliance (Mandatory Since December 2024)

The General Product Safety Regulation requires:

- [ ] **Responsible person identified** on every listing — your business name, postal address, email. Fill in Etsy's GPSR fields.
- [ ] **Product traceability** — batch number, serial number, or individual identification (for unique pieces: the signed piece itself suffices).
- [ ] **Risk assessment documented** — especially for garden decor with sharp edges, chemical treatments, or outdoor hazards.
- [ ] **Technical documentation available** — materials used, safety considerations.

**For handmade sculpture/art:** your business contact details as responsible person + signed/numbered pieces for traceability is usually sufficient. Garden items with potential hazards (sharp metal, treated wood) need more thorough documentation.

**Non-compliance risk:** listings can be hidden from EU buyers or flagged by Etsy.

## Packaging Waste Regulations

If you ship to these countries, you must register:

| Country | System | What to Do | Cost |
|---------|--------|-----------|------|
| **Germany** | LUCID register + dual system (Der Grüne Punkt, etc.) | Register at verpackungsregister.org. Contract with a dual system. | Free registration, ~20-100 EUR/year for dual system |
| **France** | CITEO or Léko | Register, obtain IDU number. Triman marking on packaging. | ~80-150 EUR/year |
| **Spain** | ENVALMA / Ecoembes | Domestic compliance. | Varies |

**Germany is the most enforced.** Since it's your #1 market, register with LUCID first. Fines up to 200,000 EUR for non-compliance.

Consider **Ecosistant** or similar services (~200-500 EUR/year) to handle multi-country EPR registration.

## EU Seasonal Calendar

| Month | EU Events | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Jan** | Reyes Magos (ES, Jan 6). French soldes d'hiver (sale season). | Gift push for Reyes. New Year refresh messaging for Northern EU. |
| **Feb** | Valentine's Day (pan-EU, moderate). | Romantic/gift decor. |
| **Mar** | Spring begins. Easter prep. | **List all garden items NOW.** Spring decor push. |
| **Apr** | Easter (varies). Koningsdag (NL, Apr 27). | Garden season peak begins. Easter decor. |
| **May** | Mother's Day (ES: 1st Sun, DE: 2nd Sun, FR: last Sun). | Three separate Mother's Day pushes for three markets. |
| **Jun** | Father's Day (3rd Sun). French soldes d'été. Summer begins. | Father's Day gifting. Summer garden peak. |
| **Jul-Aug** | **SUMMER SLOWDOWN** — significant across Southern/Central EU. | Create inventory. Refresh listings. Batch content for autumn. |
| **Sep** | La Rentrée (FR) — home refresh season. | "Back to life" messaging, home decor push. |
| **Oct** | Halloween (growing in EU). Garden decor second peak. | Fall garden. Halloween decor (moderate). |
| **Nov** | Black Friday/Cyber Monday (adopted pan-EU, often "Black Week"). Advent prep (DE). | Run sale during Black Week. List Christmas items. |
| **Dec** | Sinterklaas (NL, Dec 5). Christmas (pan-EU). | Peak season. Post clear shipping deadlines per country. |

### Key Differences from US

- **No Thanksgiving** — Christmas shopping starts earlier (Oct-Nov) and is more spread out
- **Summer is dead** — Jul-Aug drops hard. Use it to prepare inventory, not to sell.
- **Spring/garden season (Mar-May) is CRITICAL** — arguably more important in EU than US. European garden culture (DE, NL, UK) is intense.
- **Mother's Day varies by country** — Spain (1st Sun May), Germany (2nd Sun May), France (last Sun May). Three separate pushes.
- **Advent is serious in Germany** — gift buying starts early November in DACH markets.
- **January sales are real** in France (soldes) — promote accordingly.

## Leveraging Your Spanish Location

### Advantages

1. **Mediterranean aesthetic is trending** in Northern European markets — terracotta, raw clay, sun-baked textures, warm earth tones. What's local to you is fashionable to them.
2. **Year-round outdoor photography** — you can shoot garden decor in natural Mediterranean light and settings year-round. Northern EU sellers can't do this from October-March.
3. **"Handmade in Spain"** carries positive connotations: artisan tradition, warm aesthetics, Mediterranean lifestyle. Use it in every listing.
4. **Lower studio costs** than Germany/France/Netherlands = better margins.
5. **Local materials** — Spanish clay, stone, and traditional materials at lower cost. Mention sourcing in descriptions.
6. **EU membership** — no customs barriers. Promote "ships from within the EU, lower carbon footprint than overseas."

### Challenges

- **Geographic periphery** — Spain is farther from the EU economic center (Germany/Benelux). Shipping is 2-3 EUR more expensive than from NL or DE.
- **Domestic market is small** — most sales will be to other EU countries.
- **Autónomo costs** — mandatory social security is relatively high for low-volume sellers.

### How to Use It

- Photograph products in your natural Mediterranean environment
- Mention "Hecho a mano en España" / "Handcrafted in Spain" prominently
- Tell the story of local materials and traditions
- Position sustainability: shorter shipping distances than overseas sellers, local sourcing
- Use warm, sun-lit photography as your signature visual style

## EU Measurements and Conventions

Always use metric first (this is opposite to US guide):

- Dimensions: "24 x 36 cm (9.4 x 14.2 inches)" — metric first
- Weight: "2.3 kg (5 lbs)" — metric first
- For UK market specifically: inches are also understood

Currency: list prices in EUR. Etsy auto-converts for UK buyers (GBP) and others.
